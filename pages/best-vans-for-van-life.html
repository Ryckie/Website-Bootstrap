Best Vans For Van Life: Sprinter Vs Transit Vs Promaster

What are the best vans for van life? Compare the budgets, off-road capability, and cargo space to narrow down the options that will work for you.

If you're reading this, chances are you're probably wondering, "what are the best vans for van life?". There are a lot of different options out there to choose from and each van model has its own pros and cons. Whether you're looking at a Mercedes Sprinter, Ford Transit, Dodge Promaster, or even a VW and budget-friendly cargo-style vans, there's a lot to consider.

Your budget, what you'll be using the van for, and the climates you hope to travel in are just a few factors that determine what the best van will be for you.

Mercedes Sprinter Vans

The Sprinter is one of the best vans for van life if it's within your budget. The #1 reason for choosing a Sprinter over other vehicle types is it's currently the only one with a factory 4x4 option.

Sprinter Van Pros

  • They get good gas mileage for a vehicle of their size (11,8 L/100km for the 2 wheel drive option, 15-16 L/100km in the 4x4 option).
  • They are available in both a 144" and 170" wheelbase for different size options.
  • They are available in diesel, which means a 600+ km lifetime.
  • Compared to the other panel vans, Sprinters have a higher payload than the Transit or the Promaster.
  • Unless you're very tall, most people can stand up inside.

Sprinter Van Cons

  • They are expensive off the bat and are expensive to maintain.
  • They operate on a complex computer system.
  • Many mechanics won't work on them, so you are stuck paying dealer prices.
  • They are prone to rust.

Is 4x4 Worth It?

Sprinters are expensive to begin with, but the 4x4 upgrade comes with an even heftier price tag. 4x4 Sprinters have a 4" higher clearance than the standard 2 wheel drive Sprinter and hold their value when it comes to resale. However, they are significantly less gas efficient than the 2wd version.

144" vs 170" Wheelbase

My first Sprinter was the 144" wheelbase and my second Sprinter was the 170" wheelbase which provides about 1,07 meter extra of living space. The two wheelbases are very similar in the way they drive and both are highly maneuverable.

Ford Transit Vans

For the first time in 2020, the Ford Transit came out in an all-wheel-drive version. It is less expensive than the Sprinter, but as of 2021, the all-wheel-drive Ford Transit only comes in a gas model.

Ford Transit Pros

  • The biggest advantages of the Ford Transit over the Sprinter are the lower cost of maintenance and the fact that you can have it serviced anywhere.
  • Parts are easy to come by.
  • Ford Transits are less expensive upfront compared to Sprinters.
  • They have the tallest interior height option.

Ford Transit Cons

  • Ford Transits have lower clearance than some of the other van options out there.
  • The new all-wheel-drive model is only available with a gas engine, not diesel.
  • Ford Transits are slightly less fuel efficient compared with Sprinters.

Dodge Promaster Vans

The Dodge Promaster is the most economical of the panel-style vans, so it's a great option for those on a budget. In contrast to the 2wd Transit and Sprinter, the Promaster is front-wheel drive which provides more traction but isn't as nimble.

Dodge Promaster Pros

  • Most economic of the panel style vans.
  • Can average about 13,1 liter per 100km on highways.
  • DIY friendly boxy shape with straight lines makes for an easier conversion.

Dodge Promaster Cons

  • The longest model is about 0,9 meter shorter than the 170" Sprinter, so the Promaster will require you to pack more thoughtfully.
  • Ground clearance on the Promaster is about 2,5 centimeter less than the 2wd Transit and Sprinter.
  • Front wheel drive means getting stuck is more common than RWD, AWD, or 4x4 vehicles.

Other Van Life Options

Ford Econoline

The E-Series is budget-friendly with a huge used market. Some have even been converted to 4 wheel drive and these are extremely off-road capable. The downside is they are gas guzzlers and you cannot stand up inside unless a pop-top has been installed.

VW Vanagon / Westfalia

These vintage-style vans offer tried and tested layouts, many with a pop-top, built-in bed, and a built-in kitchen. However, parts are hard to come by and with the age of the vehicle, you are bound to run into issues.

Questions To Ask Yourself

There is no one size fits all solution when it comes to choosing the right vehicle for van life. Here are a few questions to ask yourself before choosing a van.

  • What is your budget? If van life imposes too much financial stress on you, you'll be missing the point altogether. The right vehicle for you is one that's within your means.
  • Part-time or Full-time? If you plan on living in your van full-time, you may want a shower, a bathroom, and a bit more space. If you're using it for traveling here and there, you might not need these things.
  • Where will you use it? If you plan to rally your van on gnarly dirt roads or sand, then 4x4 may be non-negotiable. If you frequently need to go stealth in the city, then you'll want something smaller and more discrete.
  • How tall are you? The Ford Transit has the most interior headspace, followed by the High Roof Sprinter. If you want to stand up inside your van, these and the Promaster are your best options.
